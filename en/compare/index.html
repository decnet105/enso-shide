Point-by-point comparison

Enso Shide vs Day One, Apple Journal, Journey, Diarium

Enso Shide is a local-first life-archive app for overseas Chinese families: core memories live on the iPhone (SwiftData), the public version needs no account, and it renders a bilingual Chinese/English A4 PDF life book on device. Its core difference is that it is organized around Chinese source text, an English reading layer, and Chinese cultural context — to help you pass a life on to a next generation that reads in English.

How is Enso Shide different from Day One or Apple Journal?

Most journals are built around single-language entries and cloud sync. Enso Shide is organized around Chinese source text, an English reading layer, and Chinese cultural context. Core memories live on the iPhone (SwiftData is the source of truth), the public version needs no account, and it renders a bilingual Chinese/English A4 PDF life book on device. It is closer to a family heirloom archive than a daily diary.

Which journals are local-first and keep the data in your hands?

Enso Shide keeps core Memory, Profile, and Keepsake in SwiftData on the iPhone as the source of truth, and the public v1 records, reviews, and makes books locally with no account. Diarium lets you choose your own cloud (OneDrive, Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud, WebDAV), so the data sits in your account. Day One and Journey are cloud-sync first, both with encryption (Day One has built-in end-to-end encryption on by default; Journey's end-to-end encryption is opt-in, and its free Google Drive tier is not end-to-end encrypted). Apple Journal records on device and syncs via iCloud.

Which one makes a bilingual book the next generation can read?

Enso Shide keeps separate Chinese and English content fields and renders a side-by-side Chinese/English A4 PDF life book on device with UIGraphicsPDFRenderer, aimed at a next generation that reads in English; the deterministic on-device path works even without AI. Day One, Journey, and Diarium can export PDF or Word / HTML / JSON, but the layout is not designed for bilingual reading; Apple Journal has no dedicated book feature, only per-entry share or print.

What if you prefer free or a one-time purchase over a subscription?

Apple Journal is free and built into iOS. Diarium is free plus a one-time Pro purchase per platform (no subscription; the Windows version is a paid app that already includes Pro). Day One has a free Basic tier; paid Silver is $49.99/year and Gold is $74.99/year. Journey has a free tier with premium around $29.99/year. Enso Shide's on-device core is usable without an account, and any subscription pricing is per the App Store.

Which is better for syncing across devices and platforms?

This is Enso Shide's current boundary, and we state it plainly: it does not offer cross-device cloud sync yet, and core memories live on a single iPhone. If you need multi-device or cross-platform sync, Day One (iOS, Mac, Android, Web), Journey (iOS, Android, desktop, Web), Diarium (Windows, Android, iPhone, iPad, Mac), or Apple Journal (iPhone, iPad, Mac) are better fits.

What are the AI features in each app?

Enso Shide offers optional online AI tidy-up and book generation that only processes current content when you trigger it; a deterministic on-device path still makes a book without AI. Day One and Apple Journal use Apple Intelligence prompts and highlights (running on device on supported models, optional). Journey offers paid Odyssey AI. Diarium focuses on smart integrations (weather, calendar, fitness) rather than generative AI.
