# How overseas families can pass Chinese life stories to the next generation

The hard part of cross-generational transmission is usually not a shortage of photos, but a shortage of people, times, places, and era context. The original Chinese text should be preserved; English content is a reading bridge and should not replace the original.

## Four kinds of information to preserve together

| Information | Purpose | Recommendation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Original Chinese text | Preserves voice and tone | Serve as the authoritative text |
| English content | Helps the next generation read | Place alongside the original |
| Times and places | Establishes life coordinates | Store in structured form |
| Era context | Explains shared memory | Cite verifiable public sources |

## Why a printed output still matters

A digital book provides a stable order, chapters, and a shared reading occasion. Ongoing records and a shareable PDF should come from the same structured memory rather than being maintained separately. Physical books are not within Ensō v1 scope.
