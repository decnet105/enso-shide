# Choosing between local-first notes, cloud journals and knowledge tools

Choosing a tool depends on your data and how you use it, not on a single ranking. Family memories, team collaboration and personal knowledge management have different requirements for sync, privacy and output.

## Comparison by task

| Dimension | Local-first | Cloud journal | Knowledge-base tool |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Authoritative copy | Device or local files | Server account | Depends on the product |
| Cross-device collaboration | Usually needs extra sync | Usually strong | Usually strong |
| Core records offline | Usually available | Verify case by case | Verify case by case |
| Printed book output | Depends on a dedicated engine | Usually an export | Usually needs a template |
| Best suited for | Private archives | Continuous journaling | Research and linking |

## Before comparing any specific product

Check the product's current official documentation, pricing page and privacy policy, and record the verification date. Features change, so an old review or user speculation should not be written up as a permanent fact.

> Enso Shide today is closer to a hybrid "local core plus networked services" architecture, and its current account requirement must also be part of any decision.
